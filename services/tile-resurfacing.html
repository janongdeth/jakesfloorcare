Tile Resurfacing

A new finish on your existing tile. No demolition, no dust, no drama.

What We Do

Refinish Worn Tile Without Tearing It Out

Your tile still works fine. It's just ugly. Maybe it's dated, maybe it's chipped, or maybe the color makes you cringe every time you walk into the bathroom. You don't need to rip it all out.

Tile resurfacing gives your existing tile a brand-new finish — new color, smooth surface, and a look that lasts years. It costs a fraction of full replacement, takes a fraction of the time, and there's no demolition mess to deal with.

We resurface bathroom tile, kitchen tile, shower surrounds, countertops, and commercial tile surfaces. If you're sick of looking at it but not ready to gut the room, this is the move.

How It Works

Our Process

01

Surface Prep

We clean, degrease, and sand the tile surface to create the right profile for the new coating to bond properly.

02

Repair

Chips, cracks, and damaged grout lines get filled and leveled so the finished surface is smooth and even.

03

Prime & Coat

A bonding primer goes down, followed by multiple coats of a durable finish in the color and sheen you choose.

04

Cure

The surface cures to a hard, moisture-resistant finish. Most projects are ready for use within 24-48 hours.

Why It Matters

Benefits of Tile Resurfacing

Fraction of Replacement Cost

Save 50-70% compared to ripping out tile and installing new. Same visual result, much smaller bill.

No Demolition

No sledgehammers, no dust everywhere, no weeks of construction noise. We work over what's already there.

Done in Days, Not Weeks

Most bathrooms are finished in 1-2 days. Full replacement can take weeks by the time you factor in lead times.

Custom Color Options

Choose from a range of colors and finishes. Update pink 1960s tile to modern white without touching the layout.

Moisture Resistant

The finished surface is sealed and moisture-resistant, making it ideal for bathrooms and kitchens.

Extends Tile Life

Adds years to your existing tile installation. A smart stop-gap before a full remodel down the road.
